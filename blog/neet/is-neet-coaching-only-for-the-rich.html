Is NEET Coaching Only for the Rich?

A dream is a dream.
It feels the same for everyone.

The fire burning inside a dream doesn’t know whether you can afford fees or manage a financial crisis. That dream might have started in childhood— playing with a toy stethoscope. Or from the fascination with a white coat. Or from a seed planted by parents long ago.

But where are many of those dreamers now?

  • Some convinced themselves that the syllabus was too vast.
  • Some were convinced by parents who simply couldn’t afford it.
  • Some dreams slowly faded.
  • Some were buried completely.

But why?

Because of the fear of failure.

Failing after spending a huge amount of money.
Failing even after writing countless mock tests.
Failing despite joining a so-called “top” institute with AIR results.

That fear breaks dreams more than the exam itself.

NEET is not for the rich.
It is for those with perseverance, consistency, and the courage to keep going.

Yes, words like hard work and perseverance sound common and boring now. But for uncommon dreams like becoming a doctor, you still need those very common qualities—used every single day.

You’ve already done the hardest part.
You dreamed.

Dreaming itself is an art.
You’ve already created your masterpiece in your heart and mind.

Don’t ruin your own art because of fear, money, or others’ opinions.

This galaxy is your art gallery.
And I hope every dream finds its place on display.

NEET is not for the rich.
It’s not for the privileged.

It’s for you.

— Team Sasthra
